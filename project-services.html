Featured Projects

GINAMA PROJECT | Pasalubong Center

Commercial Building

The concept of the design strives towards a cultural approach but the client wanted it to be modernized so the architect incorporated both concepts. There are printed patterns on the vertical louvers on the front that represents weaving designs and the vertical represents the modern design.

NUNEZ PROJECT

Single-Story Residential Building

This is owned by a relative close to the architect's mother's side that she personally design for them. The design was inspired by the already existing slope of the roof and wooden louver windows. Basically, this was just a total renovation of the existing house bwith some twists and turns for a new look.

CAINGLET PROJECT

Single-Story Residential Building

This Project was one of the first project that the architect handled during her junior years. It has a roof deck but not the whole area of the ground floor. The design was all Modern but the client specifically wanted Jalousie as their windows and it did blended well.

COFFEESHOP PROJECT

Interior Design

This was a coffee shop designed in line with the client's sport, tri-athlete. It was fun designing this 25 sqm area since the client wanted to place his bicycle inside the coffee shop and it was a lot of work considering the space available. Still, we nailed it.

About me


Hi! I am Architect Marie Joenaren.


The question of why I chose to study architecture is often asked, but the answer doesn't stem from a specific moment of realization. In fact, pursuing architecture has been a lifelong aspiration for me. The desire to create and design spaces has been ingrained in me from a young age, and I've always known that this was the field I wanted to pursue. So, when asked why I studied architecture, the answer is simple: it's been my lifelong pursuit.

Over the years, my experience in the field of architecture has allowed me to witness the vast diversity of this profession. It's been truly awe-inspiring to see how architecture can have such a significant impact on the culture and history of the places it inhabits. This realization has instilled in me a great sense of pride and responsibility as an architect. To continue to grow and excel in my chosen profession, I am actively seeking out opportunities to learn from experienced mentors and develop my skills as a new architect.

Today, I am a Cagayan de Oro-based architectural designer, illustrator, and artist. I take every bit of inspiration from my childhood into my everyday work.

I don't just design spaces - I tell stories through them.
